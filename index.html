Secret combinations arise. Criminal networks and corrupt collusion undermining rule of law. Prophetic warning that secret combinations will be among all nations. Helaman 6; Helaman 7; Helaman 2; Ether 8
Pride cycle: prosperity → pride → destruction → humility → prosperity. Economic booms followed by complacency, inequality, polarization, and social fracture. Persistent pride hastens cleansing judgments; repentance brings preservation. Helaman 12; 3 Nephi 6; Alma 62; 3 Nephi 9
Prophets warn despite ridicule (Samuel, Nephi & Lehi). Living prophets warn of moral decline; public skepticism and mockery. Rejection precedes cleansing judgments before the Second Coming. Helaman 13; Helaman 14; Helaman 15; Helaman 16; Helaman 5

Korihor (anti‑Christ) denies Christ and morality. Secular ideologies deny sin, prophecy, and accountability. Voices against God are silenced at His coming. Alma 30
Nehor introduces priestcraft (paid popularity‑based religion). Commercialized spirituality; flattery-based teaching for gain. Priestcraft collapses; only Christ’s doctrine endures. Alma 1

King-men seek to overthrow liberty and the voice of the people. Power grabs and factionalism undermine inspired checks and balances. Liberty preserved only through righteousness. Alma 2; Alma 51; Ether 2; 2 Nephi 1
Title of Liberty—covenantal defense of family, religion, and freedom. Righteous civic engagement to defend faith and family. Remnant upholds liberty preparatory to Zion. Alma 46
Captain Moroni’s fortifications and preparations. Build spiritual and temporal defenses at home and in community. Those built on Christ’s rock withstand the storms. Alma 49; Alma 50; Helaman 5:12
Inequality corrodes unity; costly apparel; class division. Wealth gaps and status tribes reduce social trust. Zion requires ‘no poor among them.’ Alma 4; 4 Nephi 1
4 Nephi’s Zion society, then gradual decline as pride returns. Seasons of unity followed by materialism and fracture. Prophesied Zion/New Jerusalem established before the Lord’s return. 4 Nephi 1
Christ ministers to the Nephites; establishes His doctrine and church. Restoration of priesthood, ordinances, and covenants; worldwide Church. Gathered Israel prepares earth for millennial reign. 3 Nephi 11; 3 Nephi 12; 3 Nephi 18; 3 Nephi 27
Gathering of Israel promised; sign of the record’s coming forth. Book of Mormon as instrument of gathering; missionary work. Israel gathered; New Jerusalem built. 3 Nephi 20; 3 Nephi 21; 1 Nephi 15
Prophecy of a ‘choice seer’ from Joseph—Joseph Smith. Restoration through Joseph Smith; translation of the Book of Mormon. Work proceeds until all nations receive testimony. 2 Nephi 3
Moroni addresses latter‑day readers; preservation of the plates. Book of Mormon speaks to skepticism, materialism, churches for gain. Those who receive the record are numbered with the covenant people. Mormon 8; Mormon 9
Churches built up to get gain; denial of miracles. Religious commercialization; rejection of continuing revelation. Lord manifests miracles among the faithful. Mormon 8; Mormon 9
Two churches only—the Lamb’s church vs. the church of the devil. Clear moral divide intensifies in the last days. Saints armed with righteousness and great glory. 1 Nephi 14
Great and abominable church removes plain and precious truths. Systems corrupt truth for power and gain; spiritual confusion. Fullness of the gospel restored. 1 Nephi 13
Land of liberty is choice when serving God; warned against captivity. Civic freedom tied to righteousness and gratitude. A righteous, covenant-based society prepares for Zion. 2 Nephi 1; 2 Nephi 10; Ether 2
Doctrine of Christ—faith, repentance, baptism, Holy Ghost, endure. Personal conversion and covenant belonging. Sanctified saints prepared to meet the Lord. 2 Nephi 31; 2 Nephi 32; 3 Nephi 11
Lamanite conversion through sons of Mosiah; miraculous turnarounds. Global missionary work and conversion among long‑separated peoples. Israel gathered from all tribes and kindreds. Alma 17; Alma 26
Societal collapse through contention, vengeance, and secret works. Cycles of civil strife and dehumanization; breakdown of norms. Final wars precede the Lord’s return; only Zion is safe. Mormon 2; Mormon 6; Ether 14; Ether 15
Cataclysm at Christ’s death; voice of mercy invites repentance. Natural and social upheavals accompanied by the Lord’s invitations. Desolations and signs precede His glory; repentant gathered. 3 Nephi 8; 3 Nephi 9; 3 Nephi 10
By small and simple means the Lord brings great things to pass; Liahona type of the word. Daily scripture and prayer guide like a spiritual compass. Faithful are led safely through the final wilderness to Zion. Alma 37
Record keeping and transmission preserve faith for future generations. Family history and temple work bind hearts across generations. Turning of hearts (Malachi) realized by sealing power. Jarom 1; Mosiah 1; 3 Nephi 25
Alma the Younger’s conversion from rebellion to prophet. Dramatic repentance and healing through Christ; recovery from bondage. Millions experience a mighty change as part of the gathering. Mosiah 27; Alma 36
King Benjamin’s covenant sermon—atonement, service, care for the poor. Christlike service, ministering, and covenant identity as ‘children of Christ.’ Zion society practices pure religion with no poor among them. Mosiah 2; Mosiah 3; Mosiah 4; Mosiah 5
Jacob condemns immorality and materialism; invites purity of heart. Pornography, infidelity, exploitation; healing through chastity and fidelity. The pure in heart see God; Zion established. Jacob 2; Jacob 3
Olive tree allegory—God grafts, nourishes, and gathers Israel. Missionary and temple work across nations; adoption into Israel. Final pruning and harvest; vineyard preserved; millennial rest. Jacob 5
Sherem denies Christ and revelation; demands a sign; is confounded. Sign-demanding unbelief and hostility to continuing revelation. Unbelief collapses before the revealed Lord; every knee bows. Jacob 7
Nephi’s psalm—wrestling with weakness; trusting in the Lord. Faith-based mental health: lament → faith → action; confidence in Christ. Overcomers sealed His; songs of everlasting joy in Zion. 2 Nephi 4
Isaiah in the Book of Mormon—enlarge the tent; no weapon prospers; nations flow to Zion. Worldwide Church growth; stakes as tent cords; covenant protection. Zion’s righteousness shines; nations flow to the Lord’s house. 2 Nephi 12; 2 Nephi 24; 3 Nephi 22
Faith, hope, and charity—the pure love of Christ never fails. Christlike character withstands cynicism and contention. Charity qualifies souls for the presence of God. Moroni 7
Moroni’s final invitation—ask with real intent; spiritual gifts. Personal witness through the Holy Ghost; gifts operate worldwide. All truth gathered in Christ; every tongue confesses He is Lord. Moroni 10
